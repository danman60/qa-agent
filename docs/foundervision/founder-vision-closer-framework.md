## The CLOSER Framework

How to structure sales calls like the top 1% sales teams.

| Letter | Step | What To Do |
|--------|------|------------|
| **C** | **Clarify** | Clarify why the person is there |
| **L** | **Label** | Label their current state, desired state, and the gap stopping them. Position yourself as the bridge from where they are to where they want to go |
| **O** | **Overview Past Pain** | Overview past pain and experiences they've tried before. Associate with what they liked about past solutions, disassociate with what they hated. Then ask "what else?" and repeat (the Pain Cycle) |
| **S** | **Sell the Vacation** | Create a three-pillar pitch. Examples: Fitness = fitness + nutrition + accountability. Realtor leads = timely + exclusive + qualified. There's always three you can chunk up or down to |
| **E** | **Explain Away Concerns** | People blame circumstances, other people, or avoid making decisions. This is where objections come up — answer them and close the sale |
| **R** | **Reinforce the Decision** | After the deal closes, make next steps clear and keep your promises. This is the handoff between the sale and when they begin. "Man, these guys are on top of it" |

## Key Concepts

- **Pain Cycle:** Overview past pain → associate with liked aspects → disassociate with hated aspects → "what else?" → repeat
- **Three-Pillar Pitch:** Always reduce your offer to three core pillars
- **Reinforce:** Not selling your way out of a sale — close once they say yes, THEN reinforce by delivering on promises

How to structure sales calls like the top 1% sales teams using the CLOSER framework.

**C — Clarify:** Clarify why the person is there.

**L — Label:** Label them with a problem — their current state, their desired state, and the gap that's stopping them from getting where they want to go. We want to position ourselves as the bridge that'll take them from where they are to where they want to go.

**O — Overview Past Pain:** You overview past pain and experiences that they have tried before you, associate with what they liked about their past solutions, and then disassociate with the things that they hated. Then go back to the top and ask "what else?" and repeat this. It's called the Pain Cycle.

**S — Sell the Vacation:** Come up with what I call a three-pillar pitch. For example, when I sold fitness, it was fitness, nutrition, and accountability — those are the three pillars. When I created a script to sell leads to realtors, it was: you want the leads to be timely, you want them to be exclusive, and you want them to be qualified. There's always three that you can chunk up or chunk down to.

**E — Explain Away Concerns:** People either blame circumstances outside, other people, or avoid making the decisions themselves altogether. So here's where they're going to bring up objections. And we want to answer them and then close the sale.

**R — Reinforce the Decision:** This isn't selling your way out of a sale. Close the deal once they say yes. After it's closed, this is the handoff between the sale and when they begin. All you have to do in this stage is make it clear what next steps will look like, and then keep your promises of making sure that those exact things happen when you said they would. So people are like, "man, these guys are on top of it."

That's the CLOSER Framework — the overarching structure for top 1% sales teams.
