## Full Transcript

Influence. S tier. Just understanding all the components of it. And I would say it's paired well with Pre-Suasion by Cialdini as a perfect wine and cheese pairing of persuasion sales.

How to Win Friends and Influence People? Also S tier. I was 18 the first time I read it. It materially changed the way I acted and performed.

The Seven Habits of Highly Effective People — for me, B tier. Didn't really do it for me. Some people, changed their lives.

Think and Grow Rich — B tier for me. I just measured like how much my life changed as a result of reading the book.

21 Laws of Leadership — A tier, really really good. The only thing that I think I had to do was take some of the words that he had and say, okay, how do I define these words into operating terms? But in terms of all of his stories and anecdotes, really good.

Ready, Fire, Aim — A tier. The period of time that I read that book, it filled in a couple of holes in my way of seeing business that actually helped me keep moving forward. So that came out at the right time for me.

$100M Leads — S tier book for most people, A tier for me. I've more read the book to understand what he did about writing the book that made it so compelling, more than the contents of the book itself. But yeah, I think obviously it's the number one nonfiction book of like five years. It's very hard to say that there's anything to knock against it.

The Bezos Letters — A tier. Each one is one really important lesson. You read these letters back from 1997 into today — he had one vision and was very flexible on how to get there, but you could see his principles shine the whole way through.

Principles — A tier. Really helps understand the learning-failure loop.

The 38 Letters from JD Rockefeller to His Son — I would say writing-wise not A tier, but in terms of lessons, S tier.

$100M Offers — S tier. Concepts completely changed how I saw valuing a business for its assets plus the story behind it.
